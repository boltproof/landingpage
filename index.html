Situation

A small office needed shared storage for project files, with two physical sites available within Ireland. They wanted shared access, but also some protection if one site was unusable.

Approach

I designed a layout using two Synology units:

  • Site A: Main NAS with SHR, shared folders and permissions for staff.
  • Site B: Secondary NAS used as an encrypted replication target.
  • Hyper Backup / Snapshot Replication between sites on a scheduled basis.
  • Tested restoring a deleted project folder directly from the remote copy.

I also documented:

  • Which folders were replicated.
  • How long snapshots were kept.
  • What to do if Site A was unavailable for an extended period.
Outcome

Without much complexity, the business achieved a practical 3‚Äì2‚Äì1 pattern:

  • Primary working copy on the PCs and the main NAS.
  • Local backup and snapshots at Site A.
  • Off-site encrypted copy at Site B.
